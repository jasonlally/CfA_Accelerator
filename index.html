OVERVIEW

“We’ve got to have the brightest minds to help solve our biggest challenges.”—President Obama presenting new management agenda

Historically, technology has been the biggest driver of productivity and innovation gains in many sectors—with government being a notable exception. The barriers to adoption of government technology are many but the most significant is a shortage of sector-specific solutions. This has left government with two primary options:

  1. use technology intended for Fortune 500 companies, or
  2. develop custom solutions that don’t scale well or easily.

However, with the emergence of cloud services, growing govtech investment and civic accelerators, the startup ecosystem is now seeing opportunity in this $140+ billion market. (See Federal IT Spending and State & Local IT Spending)

The nascent govtech ecosystem is missing one critical element customer insight of pain points and an understanding of government mechanics. Through the San Francisco Entrepreneurship in Residence program (SF EIR), the Mayor's Office of Civic Innovation seeks to bridge this gap by connecting public-sector staff directly with entrepreneurs to seed problem discovery and product development. With thousands of pain points, government has the potential to support a large ecosystem of best-of-breed solutions helping catalyze public sector innovation and productivity.

PROGRAM

SF EIR is the only government EIR program in the country. We offer each accepted team office space and senior mentors at City Hall for four months, and most importantly, access to a gold-mine of government problems and insights through the City and County of SF.

What We Offer

Direct access to and assistance from the customer—civil servants in search of solutions to real problems

Workshops and trainings on important topics related to working with government like open data standards, procurement, and security

Mentorship from senior public sector leaders across the Bay Area and private sector leaders working in a diversity of industries

Access to space in City Hall if you need it

A high profile stage from which to offer your product to other local government customers

A commitment to your success

What We're Looking For

  1. Your product or service should address areas of topical relevance to the public sector (e.g. related to data, healthcare, transportation, energy, infrastructure, mobile and civic engagement, etc.)
  2. Your team should be pursuing an opportunity that has large economic potential ($100M+ market) and significant impact potential (increased revenue, enhanced productivity, meaningful cost savings, etc.).
  3. Your product or service should be technology enabled and have broad applicability across SF and other cities and states and across public and private sector customers.
  4. Preference is given for products or services in advanced stages of development and rollout or existing products that could also address government needs. If you are a position to build out the product or service within 16 weeks, that is fine as well.
  5. Your product shows promise to be self-sustainable.
  6. Your team demonstrate high likelihood of success based on prior professional and personal experiences.
  7. Preference for teams over sole individuals; if sole individual, please elaborate on how you will build team and accomplish program objectives within 16 weeks.

What You Can Expect

The selection process will be through an open and competitive application process. Applications will be used to assess the qualifications, fit and likelihood of success of the applicants.

Selection will be made by SF EIR selection group composed of public and private sector leaders with innovation, Fortune 500, startup and venture capital experience.

Why is the program unique?

This is the first EIR program within government, who is by far the largest customer of products and services in the nation. Entrepreneurs get to work within San Francisco City Hall and work on opportunities that are actual pain points and needs of the government. In addition, products and services that successfully solve issues faced by San Francisco can easily expand to addressing similar needs of other cities and states across the nation in addition to the private sector.

Who is the target entrepreneur?

We are looking particularly for entrepreneurial teams who recognize that challenge and opportunity go hand-in-hand. It is also critical that the EIRs think about solving problems at a large scale. Pain points and issues faced by government need products and services that can scale well and can function across multiple environments and across different types of users. And we want EIRs who think big! As an analogy, we’re looking for teams who instead of building an app that tells you when the train is delayed, builds products or services that helps the trains run on time.
